Two Friends. One Bad Hand. Infinite BBQ.

Not every restaurant is born from a business plan. Jack Deuce came from two immigrants, a deck of cards, and a burning desire to feed people — and win at poker. So far, only one of those is going well.

They met years ago at a smoky underground game in a city that no longer exists on any official map. Both fresh off the plane, both bluffing with confidence they hadn’t earned yet. They played hard, bet harder, and bonded over a shared inability to ever fold — especially not Jack Deuce.

Why that hand? No one knows. Not even them. But if superstition is a spice, they were already halfway to Korean BBQ.

Over time, they swapped out card tables for prep tables and casinos for kitchens. Their dream? To bring authentic Korean BBQ to a place that didn’t know it needed it — Cherokee, North Carolina. Why Cherokee? Simple:

  • There’s a great casino
  • A lack of grilled meat
  • And the mountains feel like home

So they set up shop across from Harrah’s, rolled up their sleeves, and got to work. No gimmicks. No shortcuts. Just charcoal, flavor, and fire. They named the place Jack Deuce — not because it made sense, but because it made them.

Now, people come from all over to try their bulgogi, sip soju, and argue about whether Jack Deuce is a curse or a gift. (Hint: if you’re eating here, it’s a gift.)

This is Jack Deuce Korean BBQ.
All in on flavor. Never folding. Always betting big.
